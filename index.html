VAJRADI VISION

What is Vajradi Vision?

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Vajradi Vision is the name of this project. The name comes from the eyes of Buddha which are half-closed. The style is referred to as the “Adamantine View” or Vajradrsti in Sanskrit. Doing some wordplay, we get Vajradi. Because my project stems around computer vision, I thought adding Vision would be a nice touch. Combining the two, we get VajradiVision.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This project strives to create a relatively cheap way to gain high quality motion capture. The end product is a high quality moving 3D model of an object that can be viewed in solidworks or blender. This technology has massive applications in the health sector, especially in developing countries who can use these models to better convey potential operations to patients or gain advice from specialists in their field.

SciFi Inspiration

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Conceptually, this project is inspired from the holograms from Star Wars. Unfortunately, directly recreating the holograms from Star Wars is near impossible, but, we can get really close using some clever tricks with Neural Radiance Fields, XBox Kinetics, KineticFusion, and recent developments in AR or solidworks/blender.
